## Feedback Capture Rules

- Sessions are observational, not guided demos.
- Capture what the evaluator does, where they hesitate, and which explanations they request.
- Keep quotes short and attributed.
- Assign severity using the table below before converting any observation into Sprint 4 backlog input.
- Record access/setup constraints separately from product observations, especially anything caused by deployment availability. TB-P1 remains outside this slice.

## Severity Rules

| Severity | Rule | Decision default |
|---|---|---|
| P0 | Blocks a core evaluator task or creates materially misleading policy interpretation. | Stop pilot-readiness claim and fix before broader pilot use. |
| P1 | Creates serious credibility, interpretability, or workflow risk with a contained fix. | Must-address Sprint 4 candidate. |
| P2 | Creates friction, confusion, or missing context but does not block evaluation. | Consider for Sprint 4 if repeated or cheap to fix. |
| P3 | Preference, polish, or nice-to-have improvement. | Record for later; do not displace roadmap priorities by default. |

## Decision Criteria

Promote an observation into Sprint 4 backlog triage when it:

- appears in more than one session;
- affects the evaluator's assigned lens;
- creates policy-interpretation or model-credibility risk;
- blocks a realistic policy workflow; or
- contradicts an assumption behind the current Sprint 4 plan.

Do not promote an observation solely because it is cosmetic, depends on TB-P1 deployment migration, requests unrelated roadmap expansion, or would change roadmap priorities without separate planning approval.
