Methodology & Model Documentation

The Uzbekistan Macroeconomic Policy Engine is an open, modular platform for evidence-based macroeconomic analysis. Each model is independently documented and calibrated to Uzbekistan's economic structure.

QPM — Quarterly Projection Model

The QPM is a calibrated New-Keynesian small open-economy model solved with the IRIS Toolbox in MATLAB. It features four core equations: IS curve (aggregate demand), hybrid New-Keynesian Phillips curve, forward-looking Taylor rule, and a modified UIP condition for the exchange rate. The model is solved using Gauss-Seidel iteration with bidirectional sweeps to handle both backward-looking (UIP) and forward-looking (IS, Phillips, Taylor) dynamics.

Key calibrated parameters: b₁ = 0.70 (output gap persistence), a₁ = 0.60 (inflation inertia), a₂ = 0.20 (marginal cost pass-through), g₂ = 1.50 (Taylor rule inflation response). Steady-state: inflation target 5%, potential growth 6%, neutral real rate 3.5%.

DFM — Dynamic Factor Model

The DFM follows Bańbura & Modugno (2014) and extracts a single latent common factor from 34 mixed-frequency indicators (monthly + quarterly GDP) using the EM algorithm with a Kalman filter/smoother. The factor captures broad economic momentum and is used to nowcast quarterly GDP up to 3 months ahead. Parameters are estimated in R and exported as a JSON file; the prediction step (Kalman filter update) runs live in the browser.

Input-Output Model — Leontief Framework

The Input-Output model is based on the official 136 × 136 Supply-Use Table (ТЗВ) for Uzbekistan 2022, compiled by the Statistics Agency. The technical coefficients matrix A and the Leontief inverse L = (I − A)⁻¹ are used to compute three types of analysis:

Demand model: ΔX = L · ΔY, where ΔY_i = ΔC_i + ΔG_i + ΔI_i + ΔX_i − ΔM_i. A sector-level final demand shock is propagated through the entire production network.
Price model: Δp = Δv · L (row-vector times L matrix). A cost-push shock in value-added (e.g. wages, energy) is transmitted to all downstream sector prices.
Linkage analysis: Rasmussen backward linkage BL_j = n·(Σ_i L_ij)/Σ_ij L_ij and forward linkage FL_i = n·(Σ_j L_ij)/Σ_ij L_ij. Sectors with BL>1 and FL>1 are classified as Key Sectors. Employment multipliers are computed using formal/informal employment data from the Labour Force Survey.

CGE Model — 1-2-3 Framework

The CGE model follows the Devarajan, Lewis & Robinson (1990) 1-2-3 model structure, calibrated to Uzbekistan's 2021 Social Accounting Matrix. It distinguishes three commodities (exports, imports, domestically produced and consumed) and two sectors (tradables and non-tradables). Supply is modelled with a CET (constant elasticity of transformation) function between domestic sales and exports; import demand follows the Armington assumption. The model endogenously determines the real exchange rate, domestic price level, and trade balance. Policy instruments include tariff rates, VAT, export subsidies, government spending, and FDI shocks.

FPP — Financial Programming & Policies (CAEM Sep 2025)

The Financial Programming & Policies model implements the IMF's Country Analysis and Evaluation Model (CAEM) framework, calibrated to CAEM Uzbekistan Sep 2025 with base year 2024 and projection horizon 2025–2027. The model comprises four linked sectors:

Real sector: GDP growth path, output gap, and an open-economy Phillips Curve where imported inflation is dominant: π_t = λ₂·π_imp + (1−λ₁−λ₂)·π_exp + λ₁·π_{t−1} + λ₃·y_gap, with λ₁=0.05 (inertia), λ₂=0.70 (imported), λ₃=0.40 (output gap), imported inflation = NER depreciation + US CPI.
External sector: Current account, reserves (months of imports adequacy), BOP identity.
Fiscal sector: Revenue, expenditure, fiscal balance, debt dynamics (Δdebt/GDP).
Monetary sector: Money demand (MV=PY), NFA+NDA=M2, velocity, policy rate. A consistency matrix flags deviations from 5% inflation target, 3-month reserve floor, and 40% debt-to-GDP ceiling.

Partial Equilibrium Model — WITS-SMART Framework

The Partial Equilibrium model uses the WITS-SMART methodology to analyse the trade effects of Uzbekistan's WTO accession across all HS chapters (full import coverage, 396,733 line items). The model estimates trade creation (TC), trade diversion (TD), consumer welfare gains, and tariff revenue changes resulting from MFN tariff liberalisation scenarios.

Trade Creation: TCij = Mij · ε · (tnew − told) / (1 + told), where ε is the import demand elasticity (default 1.27), M is the baseline import value, and t is the ad-valorem tariff rate.
Trade Diversion: TDij = TCij · σ / (1 + σ), where σ = 1.5 is the substitution elasticity between FTA and MFN sources. Applied only to FTA partner rows.
Consumer Welfare: W = −TC · (tnew − told) / 2 (triangular area under demand curve).
Tariff Revenue: ΔRev = Mnew · tnew − Mold · told.

Three pre-set scenarios: 20% uniform MFN cut, 50% cut, full elimination. A custom scenario simulator allows arbitrary tariff cuts by HS chapter, section, or globally. Results are aggregated by HS section (21 sections), HS chapter (97), and partner country (211). Key 2025 baseline: total imports $42.1 bn, TC $450M, TD $207M, welfare gain $226M, revenue change −$284M.
